MForesight's Vision
MForesight: Alliance for Manufacturing Foresight serves as a think-and-do tank to forecast next generation technologies that will lead to future U.S.-based manufacturing. MForesight will focus on providing intelligence to the U.S. government and the broader manufacturing community regarding emerging technologies and opportunities for public-private investments in advanced manufacturing. MForesight also seeks to promote technology innovation to bridge the gap between basic science and manufacturers.

MFORESIGHT'S MISSION

Serve

as a catalyst and a voice of America’s advanced manufacturing community

Engage

experts from industry and academia to identify opportunities and challenges related to emerging technologies

Provide

a continuous source of intelligence to federal science and technology agencies about challenges and opportunities in the manufacturing sector.

Align

public and private research activities with important national priorities

To innovate and to revitalize U.S. advanced manufacturing, we must ensure that the promising discoveries and inventions made by researchers in U.S. are matured and manufactured in the U.S., not left to chance or passively allowed to take root elsewhere.

Stakeholders for our work include federal agencies and congressional offices; industry; and academia and research labs to promote university/industry summer camps for middle and high school students, industry apprenticeships and internships, and focused programs in manufacturing in four-year colleges.

History

MForesight was established in October 2015 - the product of a joint NSF-NIST solicitation. The May 2015 solicitation was created upon recommendation by the President’s Council of Advisors on Science and Technology (“Report to the President: Accelerating U.S. Advanced Manufacturing” PDF), and its purpose was to establish a national consortium to provide coordinated private‐sector input on national advanced manufacturing technology research and development priorities. Drs. Sridhar Kota and Lawrence Molnar from the University of Michigan, Ann Arbor jointly submitted their vision of what would become MForesight and their proposal was selected to carry out this important work.
